# Chess Coaching Notes

When evaluating a position, start with material balance, king safety, piece activity, and center control.

In the opening, avoid moving the same piece repeatedly without tactical reason. Develop minor pieces, contest the center, and castle early when safe.

In the middlegame, look for forcing moves first: checks, captures, and direct threats.

In the endgame, king activity rises in value and pawn structure often determines the winning plan.

A sound explanation should distinguish between tactical necessity and positional preference.
